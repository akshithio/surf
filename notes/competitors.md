# Competitor Model Results

This is the project scoreboard for the models SURF must beat. Numbers are reported by the cited sources unless marked otherwise. Keep this file scoped to reproduced or source-backed benchmark evidence, not aspirations.

## Mission

SURF should become a JEPA-based agricultural Earth observation foundation model that is competitive with or better than current remote sensing foundation models on public benchmarks, especially time-series and agriculture tasks.

Do not anchor only on Presto. Presto is the closest current CropHarvest-style pixel time-series target, but the competitor field now includes stronger broad multimodal systems, agriculture-specialized models, and released embedding-field products.

## Current Competitor Pressure Map

Latest web refresh: 2026-05-16.

| Competitor | Why it raises the bar | Concrete pressure on SURF |
|---|---|---|
| `OlmoEarth` | Open multimodal spatiotemporal model. Reports best embedding performance on `15 / 24` tasks and best fine-tuning on `19 / 29`; explicitly evaluates `BreizhCrops`, `CropHarvest-PRC`, `CropHarvest-Togo`, `PASTIS`, and a Nandi County crop-type task. | This is now one of the most important practical competitors. We need exact same-split reproduction or at least a direct comparison on CropHarvest/Togo-style transfer and PASTIS-like segmentation. |
| `AlphaEarth Foundations` | Released annual 10 m embedding fields from 2017-2024; targets sparse-label global mapping. Reports `23.9%` average error reduction against the next-best featurization approach and includes Africa crop mask, Canada crop type, and Ethiopia crop type evaluations. | AEF is the strongest sparse-label mapping pressure. It is not a trainable open model, but its embeddings are a serious baseline for agricultural sparse-label transfer. |
| `AgriFM` | Agriculture-specific multi-source temporal model pretrained on over `25M` image samples and evaluated on agricultural land mapping, field boundaries, land use/cover, paddy rice, and winter wheat. | Closest agriculture-specialized benchmark family. We need either a reproduced downstream task or an intentionally different task where pixel/temporal robustness matters. |
| `AnySat` | JEPA-based multimodal EO model built for many resolutions, scales, and modalities. Pretrains on GeoPlex, a 5-dataset / 11-sensor collection, and reports strong crop, flood, burn scar, deforestation, tree species, and land-cover transfer. | This occupies the generic "JEPA for heterogeneous EO" lane. SURF's wedge needs to be agriculture-specific temporal/phenological behavior, climate context, and missing-sensor robustness, not just "JEPA + multimodal." |
| `TerraMind` | Any-to-any generative multimodal EO model over nine modalities. Pretrained on TerraMesh: about `9M` globally distributed samples and `500B` tokens; reports top PANGAEA performance and strong few-shot/zero-shot behavior. | Raises the missing-modality/generative bar. SURF needs a focused agriculture/phenology advantage, not just "multimodal." |
| `TerraFM` | S1/S2 self-supervised model with globally distributed Sentinel-1/2 training, large spatial tiles, land-cover-aware sampling, adaptive cross-attention fusion, and local-global contrastive learning. Reports strong GEO-Bench and Copernicus-Bench results, including `m-SA-Crop-Type` and `m-Cashew-Plant`. | Direct pressure on multisensor Sentinel-1/2 representation quality, especially crop-type segmentation/classification. |
| `Copernicus-FM` | Unified Sentinel-mission model: Copernicus-Pretrain has `18.7M` aligned images from all major Copernicus Sentinel missions; Copernicus-Bench has `15` downstream tasks. | Sets the bar for modality-agnostic Sentinel handling and broad Copernicus evaluation. |
| `THOR` | Compute-adaptive Sentinel-1/2/3 model with randomized patch and image sizes, native 10m-1000m resolution handling, and PANGAEA data-limited emphasis. | Occupies the flexible-resolution / compute-accuracy trade-off lane. | `Prithvi-EO-2.0` | Strong HLS optical foundation model with crop/PASTIS/Sen4Map results. | Still important for multi-temporal optical + segmentation targets, even if not pixel-time-series aligned. |
| `Galileo` | Broad multimodal model with optical, radar, elevation, weather, location, population, etc. | Important because it overlaps our modality mix. Need to know whether our temporal/phenology objective wins where Galileo is weaker. |
| `SpectralEarth / hyperspectral crop models` | Hyperspectral crop mapping has a separate frontier; a recent cereal crop benchmark reports SpectralEarth-style pretraining far ahead of HyperSIGMA/DOFA on that specific hyperspectral setup. | Not an immediate baseline for Sentinel-only runs, but it warns that spectral richness can dominate if we later move to crop stress or yield proxies. |

## Positioning Read

The current landscape is already crowded in broad multimodal EO. The open lane is narrower and more useful:

| Already occupied | Models | What they already do | SURF positioning that still has room |
|---|---|---|---|
| Generic multimodal EO foundation modeling | Galileo, OlmoEarth, TerraMind, Copernicus-FM, TerraFM, THOR | Many modalities, flexible inputs, local/global objectives, cross-modal training, broad benchmark suites. | Do not sell generic multimodality as the main novelty. Use it as infrastructure for agricultural temporal robustness. |
| JEPA-style EO | AnySat, OlmoEarth-adjacent latent MIM, CROMA hybrid feature prediction | Feature/latent prediction, radar-optical fusion, heterogeneous sensor support. | The claim has to be phenology-aware JEPA plus robustness under agricultural failure modes, not "first JEPA for EO." |
| Crop-specific temporal modeling | Presto, AgriFM, WorldCereal/Presto deployment work | Pixel time series, agriculture mapping tasks, low-compute deployment, multi-source crop mapping. | Beat them on sparse-label, cross-region/dataset transfer, sensor dropout, and irregular temporal sampling, while preserving raw crop-discriminative cues. |
| Sparse-label global mapping | AlphaEarth Foundations | Annual 10 m embedding fields from 2017-2024 and strong sparse-label map production. | Show trainable / seasonal / time-sensitive crop monitoring behavior that annual fixed embeddings cannot easily provide. |
| Hyperspectral crop representation | SpectralEarth and cereal crop benchmark | Spectral richness can dominate crop-type mapping when HSI is available. | For Sentinel-only experiments, preserve red-edge/NIR/SWIR cues explicitly; later stress/yield work should consider whether Sentinel bands are enough. |
| Evaluation standardization critique | No One Knows the State of the Art in Geospatial Foundation Models | Shows GFM comparisons are often inconsistent across protocols, data, weights, and variance reporting. | Our eval should be boringly rigorous: copied-vs-rerun annotations, shared splits, variance, ablations for data vs architecture vs objective, and source-validation thresholding. |

Practical claim to fame to aim for:

> A crop-focused spatiotemporal JEPA that learns from S1/S2/climate/terrain inputs, preserves agronomic raw cues, and improves representation robustness under sparse labels, missing sensors, and irregular phenology-aware sampling.

## Comparison Discipline

This section preserves the useful guidance from the April 13 reference note.

Most competitor sources report downstream metrics such as F1, OA, mAP, RMSE, AUC, mIoU, or sparse-label error reduction. SURF's early runs mostly reported self-supervised validation loss under stress conditions such as sensor-off and timestep-drop settings. Those losses are useful for selecting model variants, but they are not directly comparable to the benchmark tables above.

The bridge from the current local evidence to competitor evidence is:
- frozen linear or kNN probing on CropHarvest-style pixel time series,
- few-shot curves over strict label budgets,
- clean and corrupted evaluation settings,
- direct copied-vs-rerun annotations for every baseline,
- variance across seeds or splits,
- separate ablations for data scale, input modalities, architecture, and objective.

The April reference's immediate probe setup is still useful as the smallest benchmark-readable conversion experiment:

| Axis | Values |
|---|---|
| Candidate checkpoints | `doy_off_p03_e20_tdropaug05`, `doy_off_p03_e20_tdropaug06_w5`, `doy_off_p03_e20_tdropaug07` |
| Label budgets | `1%`, `5%`, `10%`, `25%`, `100%` |
| Stress conditions | `clean`, `sensor_off_s2`, `sensor_off_s1`, `sensor_off_climate`, `temporal_drop_30`, `temporal_drop_50` |
| Classification metrics | F1 and AUROC |
| Regression proxy metrics, if used | RMSE and rank correlation |

Interpretation rule: validation-loss robustness is an internal model-selection signal. Competitor positioning starts only when that robustness turns into better downstream behavior under the same label budgets and corruptions.
